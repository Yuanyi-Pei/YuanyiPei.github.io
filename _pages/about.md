I am an MS candidate in Biomedical Sciences at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. I received my BSc in Biological Sciences from China Agricultural University.

My research focuses on sleep neurobiology and hypothalamic circuits, particularly the role of orexinergic neurons in regulating arousal, motor control, and sleep homeostasis. I am interested in understanding how sleep-dependent neural mechanisms shape behavior and how dysfunction in these systems contributes to neurodegenerative disorders such as Alzheimer’s disease.

At the Chinese Institute for Brain Research (CIBR), I worked on optimizing compact human prepro-orexin promoters for AAV-mediated targeting of orexin neurons, aiming to improve viral packaging efficiency while maintaining high specificity and coverage. I have also investigated the neural circuits linking sleep pressure with motor nuclei controlling eyelid closure using retrograde tracing and immunohistochemistry.

Currently, I am preparing for thesis research involving in vivo electrophysiology and behavioral analysis in sleep models. My long-term goal is to uncover systems-level mechanisms underlying sleep regulation and circuit dysfunction.
