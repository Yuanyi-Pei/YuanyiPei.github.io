## 1. Compact Human Prepro-Orexin Promoter Optimization

This project addressed a key technical limitation in sleep circuit research: the AAV packaging constraint imposed by the 3.2 kb human prepro-orexin promoter.

I designed truncated promoter variants by strategically removing Alu repeat regions while preserving essential regulatory elements (OE1/OE2). Using systemic AAV2/PHP.eB delivery and quantitative immunohistochemical validation, I demonstrated that compact variants (2.2 kb and 0.4 kb) significantly improved neuronal coverage while maintaining high specificity in the lateral hypothalamus.

This work expanded the effective design space for circuit-level manipulation in wild-type sleep models and is currently in manuscript preparation.

## 2. Neural Regulation of Eyelid Closure During Sleep

My undergraduate thesis investigated how sleep pressure interacts with motor nuclei controlling eyelid closure.

Using retrograde tracing from the orbicularis oculi muscle, I mapped facial motor neuron distribution (7N) and characterized their cholinergic identity through immunohistochemistry. I established a precise facial muscle injection protocol in mice and identified clustered motor neuron populations in the lateral posterior facial nucleus.

This work reflects my broader interest in how sleep-dependent neural dynamics shape motor and cognitive systems.
